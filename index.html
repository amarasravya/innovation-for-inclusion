About Us

In the 2000s, the Indian deaf community advocated for an institute focused on ISL teaching and research. The 11th Five Year Plan (2007-2012) acknowledged the neglect of people with hearing disabilities and proposed the creation of a sign language research and training center.

The Indian Sign Language Research and Training Center (ISLRTC) was initially approved in 2011 as an autonomous center under IGNOU, Delhi. However, it was closed in 2013. In 2015, the Ministry attempted to integrate ISLRTC with the Ali Yavar Jung National Institute of Hearing Handicapped (AYJNIHH), but protests from the deaf community led to the decision being overturned.

On 22nd September 2015, the Union Cabinet approved ISLRTC as an independent society under the Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities, MSJE. The final establishment order was issued on 28th September 2015.

As per the 2011 Census, India had about 50 lakh deaf persons, but ISL was still not widely used in education. There was an urgent need for ISL-trained teachers, interpreters, and better learning materials. India had less than 300 certified ISL interpreters, which was insufficient for the community‚Äôs needs.

After years of advocacy, ISLRTC was established to bridge these gaps, promote sign language, and ensure equal opportunities for the deaf community.

Planning for Accessible Events

When planning for accessible events, institutions should:

  • Determine funding policy and procedures for accommodation requests prior to receiving any requests.
  • Provide both speech-to-text and interpreting services for large public events without the need for formal requests.
  • Use assistive listening devices at events with information on how to access them.
  • Ensure all media used during events or programs are captioned.
